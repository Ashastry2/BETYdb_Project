About

TRY is a network of vegetation scientists headed by Future Earth and the Max Planck Institute for Biogeochemistry, providing a global database of curated plant traits.

Plant traits - morphological, anatomical, biochemical, physiological or phenological features of individuals or their component organs or tissues - are a key to understanding and predicting the adaptation of ecosystems in the face of biodiversity loss and global change. In 2007 the TRY project was initiated to improve the empirical data basis for such projections, aimed at bringing together the different plant trait databases worldwide. TRY version 5 (released in March 2019) contains almost 11 million trait records for 2100 traits of 4 million individual plants, representing 160,000 plant taxa (mostly species). About halve of the data are geo-referenced, providing a global coverage of more than 15,000 measurement sites.
